# Task 5: Cybercrime Case Study and Prevention Checklist

## Part A: Case Study – UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most rapidly increasing cybercrimes in India, mainly due to the widespread use of digital payment platforms. In this type of fraud, scammers manipulate individuals into authorizing transactions or revealing sensitive banking details, leading to immediate financial loss.

A typical UPI fraud follows a step-by-step pattern. First, the scammer approaches the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media platform, often pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or an interested buyer/seller. Next, they build trust or create urgency by claiming there is an issue with the victim’s account, a pending refund, or a payment to be received. The victim is then instructed to share an OTP, click on a suspicious link, or approve a “collect request” on their UPI application. Due to lack of awareness or panic, the victim unknowingly authorizes the request. Once approved, the money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account, and recovery becomes difficult.

College students, young adults, and new users of digital payments are the most common targets, as they frequently use UPI apps and may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and reduced trust in online transactions. This case highlights the importance of digital awareness, verifying payment requests, and never sharing confidential details to prevent such frauds.
